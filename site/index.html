GenAI in UX Research

Systematic Literature Review · 2023–2025 · 15 papers · April 2026

Executive Synthesis

Methods

Hybrid human–AI approaches consistently outperform either alone. AI accelerates transcript coding and thematic analysis but risks interpretive fidelity. Most mature category in the literature.

Impact

Productivity gains are real, but de-skilling, cognitive offloading, and erosion of empathy-building are credible risks. PM/UXR trust asymmetry is a structural tension in cross-functional teams.

Governance

Most underdeveloped category. No UXR-specific governance framework exists. Organizations rely on informal caution; practitioners improvise ethical guardrails in real time.

Open Questions & Gaps

Methodological

  • —No longitudinal research tracking researcher expertise over time
  • —Most studies use controlled settings; naturalistic UX practice understudied
  • —No agreed metrics for insight quality beyond efficiency

Population / Context

  • —Non-Western, non-English-language contexts nearly absent
  • —Small teams and solo practitioners underrepresented
  • —Healthcare, safety-critical, and accessibility UX unstudied

Conceptual / Theoretical

  • —No UXR-specific governance framework exists
  • —What does UXR expertise look like under sustained AI adoption?
  • —Trust calibration as a skill — no model for how it develops
